> "While poorly implemented links can cause minor annoyances for users, improper interactive links can often lead to a loss of users.
> That’s why, if you have interactive links on a website or application, it helps to include detailed context. This avoids incorrect actions being triggered or users second-guessing themselves and needing support. You can achieve this through what I call “invisible hand-holding”. It’s not as aggressive as having forced tutorials or treating your customers like idiots, but still provides them with a sense of guidance." by **Bradley Taunt**.
